\begin{resumo}[Abstract]
 \begin{otherlanguage*}{english}
   Forecasting financial market movements constitutes a challenge of great academic and economic relevance, driving the development of models that combine high predictive capability with decision-making transparency. This work developed and evaluated Deep Learning models for directional forecasting of the S\&P~500 index, integrating Explainable Artificial Intelligence (xAI) techniques to make transparent the factors that influence model decisions. Three models were implemented on daily historical data from 2000 to 2024: an LSTM (\textit{Long Short-Term Memory}) used as a baseline in an ablation study of six preprocessing variants (combining input representations and normalization strategies), and two state-of-the-art models (SOTA), represented by xLSTM-TS and Transformer-TS, which sought to predict the absolute value of the next day and derive the direction from that. From this, the ablation study identified denoising via Discrete Wavelet Transform (DWT) as the most determining factor for directional prediction. The SOTA models achieved superior directional accuracy compared to the LSTM baseline, although with absolute value regression metrics below persistence, demonstrating that error metrics are not the most suitable for evaluating financial viability. Interpretability analysis via SHAP (\textit{SHapley Additive exPlanations}) revealed that all models concentrate their attention on the most recent timesteps of the input window, regardless of its size, suggesting that smaller windows may produce equivalent results at lower computational cost.

   \vspace{\onelineskip}
 
   \noindent 
   \textbf{Key-words}: LSTM, xLSTM, Transformer, financial time series forecasting, xAI, SHAP, S\&P~500.
 \end{otherlanguage*}
\end{resumo}
